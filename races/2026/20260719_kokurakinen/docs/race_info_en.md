# Kokura Racecourse Turf 2000m Course Details

## Basic Information
- **Racecourse**: Kokura Racecourse
- **Distance**: Turf 2000m
- **Surface**: Turf
- **Average Handicap Weight**: 54.8 kg
- **Characteristics**: Small turning radius course with varied layout

## Course Explanation

### Starting Point
- **Location**: Straight pocket in front of the grandstand
- **Characteristics**: Straight start

### Start to Corner
- **Distance**: 472m
- **Characteristics**: 
  - Long distance before the first corner, so extremely fast pace is rare
  - Long straight running distance results in faster pace than 1800m races
  - Inner gate positions are not necessarily advantageous; **middle gates tend to be favored**
  - Position acquisition before the first corner is crucial

### First Corner to Homestretch Turn
- **Characteristics**: 
  - Settled pace immediately after start
  - Pace tends to stabilize in the homestretch turn
  
### Second Corner Area
- **Characteristics**: Quick cornering speeds typical of small turning radius courses

### Third Corner Area
- **Pace Change**: Pace picks up again from here
- **Competition**: Fierce battles between front-runners develop based on field composition and track conditions
- **Lap Times**: Lap times can vary significantly

### Homestretch to Fourth Corner
- **Distance**: Adequately long
- **Pace**: Acceleration phase

## Winning Post Straight

### Straight Distance
- **Distance**: 291m
- **Characteristics**: Relatively short straight

### Straight Running Characteristics
- **Elevation Changes**: Gradual undulations present
- **Visibility**: Clear line of sight from grandstand

## Betting Strategy by Ability Class

### Trends by Class

#### 3-Year-Old Maiden & 5 Million Yen Old Horse Class
- **Theory**: Following Kokura theory, **front-runners have an advantage**
- **Reason**: Horses lacking bottom strength find it difficult to close out
- **Number of Races**: High frequency of races in this class increases data reliability

#### When Class Increases
- **Pace**: Tempo tends to be faster
- **Result**: In this case, closers and late-runners can also win
- **Development**: Running style effectiveness changes based on field level

### Running Style Aptitude

#### Front-Runners
- **Advantageous Conditions**: Frequently held class races (3-year-old maiden, 5 million yen old horse)
- **Reason**: Course configuration and homestretch pace change favor front-runners
- **Note**: Better positioning than closers and late-runners

#### Closers & Late-Runners
- **Difficulty Level**: Horses lacking bottom strength struggle to close out
- **Conditions**: Effective in higher classes where fields are limited
- **Characteristics**: Short straight (291m) limits acceleration power display

## Track Condition Impact

### Relationship with Track Condition
- **Front-Runner Advantage Theory**: Battle intensity around the third corner varies with track condition
- **Lap Time Variation**: Poor track conditions can cause significant lap time fluctuations around the third corner

## Summary

Kokura Racecourse's turf 2000m is known as a **front-runner advantageous course**. This tendency is particularly pronounced in lower classes, where horses maintaining forward positions outperform closers and late-runners. With a short straight and undulating terrain, there is insufficient room to close from behind, and horses able to maintain their own pace tend to succeed. Understanding this course bias is essential for accurate predictions.
