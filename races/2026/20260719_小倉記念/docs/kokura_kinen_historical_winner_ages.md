# Kokura Kinen Historical Winner Ages

## Purpose

This document summarizes the ages of Kokura Kinen winners from 1986 through 2025. It is intended to support age-based trend analysis for the 2026 Kokura Kinen.

## Scope and Age Notation

- Period covered: 1986–2025
- Number of races: 40
- Ages are shown using the current Japanese age-counting convention.
- The 3-year-old winners in 1991 and 1996 were recorded as 4-year-olds under the old Japanese notation used at that time.

## Key Findings

- Five-year-olds have produced the most winners, with 17 victories from 40 races.
- Four-year-olds rank second, with 12 victories.
- Horses aged four or five account for 29 of the 40 winners, or 72.5%.
- Winners aged seven or older are uncommon, but four such victories occurred during the period.
- Only two 3-year-olds won during the period: Nice Nature in 1991 and Hishi Natalie in 1996.

## Analytical Use for the 2026 Race

Age should be treated as a supporting factor rather than a standalone elimination rule. The historical distribution favors four- and five-year-old horses, but older horses should remain eligible when their recent form, handicap weight, course suitability, pace profile, and expected value are strong.
